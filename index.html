M.S. Dhoni was born on July 7, 1981 in Ranchi, Bihar (now Jharkhand), India. Indian cricketer whose rise to prominence in the early 21st century culminated in his captaincy of the Indian national team that won the one-day Cricket World Cup in 2011. Dhoni made his international debut in 2004. His talent with the bat came to the fore in an innings of 148 runs against Pakistan in his fifth international match. Within a year he joined the India Test team, where he quickly established himself with a century where he scored 183 * against Pakistan. Despite his inexperience, Dhoni took over the captaincy of the one-day side in 2007 and led India to the Twenty20 (T20) world title. Series wins over Australia and Sri Lanka, among others, moved India to the top of the International Cricket Council (ICC) Test rankings for the first time in December 2009. Dhoni was honoured for his play with the ICC One Day International Player of the Year Award in 2008 and 2009. In the 2011 one-day World Cup, Dhoni's dashing innings of 91 not out—in front of a home crowd in Mumbai—paved the way for India's victory over Sri Lanka in the final. He also led India to an appearance in the semifinals of the 2015 Cricket World Cup. Dhoni stopped serving as India's captain in 2017, having led his country in 331 international matches, the most for a captain in the sport's history. Three years later he retired from international competition.

MS Dhoni has a remarkable career in the Indian Premier League (IPL). He is best known for leading the Chennai Super Kings (CSK) to multiple IPL titles. Here are some key points about his IPL career:

Team: MS Dhoni has been associated with the Chennai Super Kings (CSK) since the inception of the IPL in 2008, except for the two seasons (2016 and 2017) when CSK was suspended.

Captaincy: Dhoni has been one of the most successful captains in IPL history, leading CSK to IPL titles in 2010, 2011, 2018, and 2021.

Batting: Dhoni is known for his finishing abilities in batting, often playing crucial innings under pressure. He has scored over 4,000 runs in the IPL with an average of around 40 and a strike rate of over 135.

Records: Dhoni holds several records in the IPL, including the most dismissals by a wicketkeeper and the most matches as captain.

Leadership: Dhoni's calm and composed leadership style has been a key factor in CSK's success. He is known for his tactical acumen and ability to make shrewd decisions on the field.

Overall, MS Dhoni's IPL career has been nothing short of legendary, making him one of the most iconic figures in the history of the tournament.

MS Dhoni, is one of the most successful and iconic cricketers in the history of Indian cricket. He is renowned for his exceptional leadership skills, calm demeanor under pressure, and ability to finish matches with his batting.

Debut: Dhoni made his international debut for India in a One Day International (ODI) match against Bangladesh in December 2004. He made an immediate impact, showcasing his aggressive batting style and excellent wicketkeeping skills.

Test Cricket: Dhoni made his Test debut against Sri Lanka in December 2005. He quickly established himself as one of the best wicketkeeper-batsmen in the world. Dhoni became the captain of the Indian Test team in 2008 and led India to the number one ranking in Test cricket.

ODI Career: Dhoni's ODI career is illustrious, to say the least. He is known for his finishing abilities, especially in high-pressure situations. Dhoni led India to victory in the 2007 ICC World Twenty20, the 2010 and 2016 Asia Cups, and the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, where he famously finished the final with a six.

T20I Career: Dhoni was instrumental in India's success in T20I cricket. He captained India to victory in the inaugural ICC World Twenty20 in 2007. Dhoni's calm approach and astute captaincy were key factors in India's triumph.

Captaincy: Dhoni is regarded as one of the greatest captains in the history of Indian cricket. He led India to numerous memorable victories and is known for his cool and collected demeanor on the field. Under his leadership, India achieved the number one ranking in Test cricket and won several ICC tournaments.

Retirement: Dhoni retired from Test cricket in December 2014, citing the strain of playing all formats of the game. He retired from international cricket entirely in August 2020, ending a glorious career that spanned over 15 years.

MS Dhoni has set numerous records in his illustrious cricketing career. Here are some of his most notable records:

Most Matches as Captain in International Cricket: Dhoni holds the record for the most matches as captain in international cricket, having captained India in 332 matches across all formats.

Most Consecutive Innings without a Duck in ODIs: Dhoni holds the record for the most consecutive innings without a duck in One Day Internationals, with 120 innings.

Most Not Outs in ODIs: Dhoni has the record for the most not outs in One Day Internationals, having remained unbeaten 84 times in his ODI career.

Most Dismissals by an Indian Wicketkeeper in ODIs: Dhoni holds the record for the most dismissals by an Indian wicketkeeper in One Day Internationals, with over 400 dismissals (catches + stumpings).

Most Matches as Captain in IPL: Dhoni holds the record for the most matches as captain in the Indian Premier League (IPL), having captained the Chennai Super Kings in over 200 matches.

Most Consecutive Sixes in International Cricket: Dhoni holds the record for hitting the most consecutive sixes in international cricket, hitting 10 sixes in a row against Pakistan in 2012.

Most Stumpings in International Cricket: Dhoni holds the record for the most stumpings by any wicketkeeper in international cricket, with over 170 stumpings.

Fastest Fifty by an Indian Wicketkeeper in Tests: Dhoni holds the record for the fastest fifty by an Indian wicketkeeper in Test cricket, scoring a half-century off just 34 balls against Australia in 2013.
